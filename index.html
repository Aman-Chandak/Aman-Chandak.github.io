Research & Projects

5G-Based UAV Localization & Navigation in GNSS-Denied Environments

M.S. Thesis · Industry-Sponsored
RISE Lab, ASU · sponsored by Honeywell Technologies | 2025 – 2026
Investigates 5G New Radio Positioning Reference Signals (PRS) as a complement to GNSS for UAV navigation. Built a software-defined-radio hardware testbed using synchronized Ettus USRPs and OpenAirInterface to demonstrate meter-level OTDOA positioning indoors. Developed a neural multipath timing-bias corrector — a residual MLP with quantile and Gated Mixture-of-Gaussians heads — trained on a 2.15M-link MATLAB ray-tracing dataset. Integrating the learned bias/variance corrections into a weighted Gauss-Newton solver reduced horizontal RMSE from 11.83 m to 2.08 m, closing most of the gap to the bandwidth-limited oracle.
Stack: PyTorch, MATLAB, Python, C++, ROS 2, OpenAirInterface, USRP, Gauss-Newton solvers, EKF, Qualcomm RB5 (ONNX/Int8).
Thesis (ProQuest) AIAA SciTech 2026 paper

Heterogeneous Multi-Robot Task Allocation with Deadlock-Free Coalitions

Re-implementation & Analysis · Dai et al., RA-L 2025
MAE 598 Multi-Robot Systems · Arizona State University | Fall 2025
Re-implemented and theoretically extended the species-traits Heterogeneous MRTA framework of Dai et al. (IEEE RA-L 2025) for post-disaster search-and-rescue. Trained the decentralized REINFORCE policy under a Constrained Flashforward Mechanism (CFM) reward shaping that prevents deadlocks during coalition formation. Added a continuous Artificial Potential Field navigation layer and produced formal Lyapunov-style proofs of deadlock avoidance and finite-time mission completion. Validated end-to-end in a custom ROS 2 (Jazzy) + Gazebo simulation with 11 heterogeneous TurtleBots and mixed-height obstacle terrain.
Stack: ROS 2 Jazzy, Gazebo, Python, PyTorch, REINFORCE, Artificial Potential Fields, Lyapunov analysis.
Report (PDF) GitHub Demonstration Video

Maximum Entropy Reinforcement Learning via Energy-Based Normalizing Flow

Re-implementation & Reproducibility Study · Chao et al., 2024
CSE 574 / EEE 598 Reinforcement Learning · Arizona State University | Fall 2025
Reproduced and analyzed the MEow framework (Chao et al., 2024), which unifies policy evaluation and improvement in MaxEnt RL through Energy-Based Normalizing Flows — eliminating the Monte Carlo approximations required by SAC/SQL. Benchmarked MEow against SAC, SQL, DDPG, TD3, and PPO on five MuJoCo locomotion tasks (Hopper, HalfCheetah, Walker2d, Ant, Humanoid). Ran multi-seed reproducibility experiments and an ablation on Learnable Reward Shifting and Shifting-based Clipped Double Q-learning.
Stack: PyTorch, MuJoCo, Stable-Baselines3.
Report (PDF)

Non-Prehensile Trajectory Optimization for Rigid-Body Manipulation

EEE 587 Optimal Control of Dynamic Systems · Arizona State University | Spring 2025
Modeled sliding, pushing, and tumbling interactions between a manipulator and rigid objects without grasping, formulating a mixed-integer optimization for contact-force scheduling in MuJoCo. Benchmarked Linear Quadratic Regulator (LQR), Model Predictive Control (MPC), and the mixed-integer planner on this hybrid dynamical system to compare tracking accuracy, robustness, and compute cost.
Stack: MuJoCo, Python, NumPy, LQR, MPC, mixed-integer programming.

Automated Tethered Drone Station — India's First Commercial Deployment

Aerial IQ, India | 2021 – 2024
Designed, built, and commercially deployed an active winch station that dynamically feeds and retrieves a power-and-data cable to a hovering UAV, enabling continuous surveillance flight times. Authored real-time PID cable-tension controllers that decouple winch dynamics from flight dynamics, and fused flight state with ground-side actions over a custom MAVLink interface on Raspberry Pi. The system was the first commercially shipped tethered drone product in India.
Stack: ArduPilot, MAVLink, PID control, Python, Raspberry Pi.
